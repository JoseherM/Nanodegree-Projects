Mexican Wolf

Considered as a symbol of war and sun by the Pre-Columbian Mexico

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus baileyi
  • Average Size: 0.60 by 1.35 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 2 to 8 years
  • Habitat: Desert & tropical biome

The Mexican Wolf is an endangered specie. They are commonly called “Lobos”, and the pelt is yellowish-gray. They have an excellent hearing, sense of smell, and highly sensitive vision. They are family-oriented animals who live in family and maintain home ranges-or territories. They mainly communicate through howling.
